The Kenyan Data Protection Act of 2019, implements Article 31 of the Constitution to protect personal data by regulating its processing, storage, and transfer. It establishes the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) to enforce compliance, mandates user consent, and grants data subjects rights to access, correct, and delete their information. Key aspects of the 2019 Act include: Core Principles: Personal data must be processed lawfully, securely, and for a specific, legitimate purpose. Data Subjects' Rights: Individuals have the right to be informed of data usage, access their data, correct false information, and object to processing. Obligations for Entities: Data controllers and processors must ensure data quality, implement security measures, and obtain consent. Cross-Border Transfers: Transferring data outside Kenya is restricted unless proof of adequate, equivalent protection safeguards is provided. Penalties: The Act allows for fines and penalties for non-compliance with the set regulations.
